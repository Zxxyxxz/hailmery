THE THREAT CATEGORIES DRIVING BREACH COSTS IN 2026
----------------------------------------------------
Prompt injection remains the #1 OWASP AI security risk. It is not a novel edge case — it is the dominant attack pattern against enterprise LLM deployments. Alongside it, jailbreaking, data exfiltration, social engineering, and model inversion make up the five core AI threat categories that security controls must address by design.

Beyond these five, eight advanced threat categories — including adversarial attacks, IP theft, business logic attacks, and context attacks — are documented and actively observed. Coordinated multi-vector campaigns are especially difficult to detect: when an attacker combines prompt injection with encoding obfuscation, a single-category detector will miss it.

Encoding obfuscation alone covers more than 26 documented attack vectors — from Base64 and Unicode manipulation to null byte injection and bidirectional text override — running up to three levels of nested encoding recursion.

NIS2 AND THE EU AI ACT: WHAT THE ENFORCEMENT TIMELINE MEANS FOR YOUR CONTROLS
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
NIS2 applies to 160,000+ entities across the EU. Non-compliance carries fines up to €10 million or 2% of global turnover. The directive requires demonstrable incident prevention and audit-ready reporting — neither of which is satisfied by post-hoc logging alone.

The EU AI Act introduces additional obligations for high-risk AI systems: data governance controls (Article 10), human oversight mechanisms (Article 14), and cybersecurity robustness (Article 15). 68% of European businesses report difficulty understanding their EU AI Act responsibilities.
