## Category

**AI-Powered Continuous Threat Exposure Management (CTEM) platform** — vendor-independent, agentic. Adjacent framing used in the docs: "Offensive Security Management," "AISecOps," "Continuous Offensive Security."

## One-line promise

**OSM is the "Collision Sensor" for cyber threats** — it analyzes the environment in real time, predicts where and how an attack will occur, and tells you how to steer away (or steers for you) before the crash.

OSM's three core functions:
1. **Estimate Attack Likelihood** — predict the probability of a specific attack via Continuous Offensive Security.
2. **Identify Attacker Methods** — detail the specific TTPs an attacker would use.
3. **Provide Clear Resolutions** — step-by-step remediation, or autonomously deploy fixes.

## The CTEM frame (Gartner)

The industry, led by **Gartner**, defined **Continuous Threat Exposure Management (CTEM)** as the new playbook — a five-step cyclical process: **Scope → Discover → Prioritize → Validate → Mobilize**. But CTEM is "a map without a car": it defines *what* to do and is silent on *how* to execute at speed and scale.

**OSM is the technological embodiment of CTEM — the vehicle with the AI engine.** OSM delivers all 5 CTEM stages seamlessly via Continuous Offensive Security and automated workflows.

## Two key analogies (use these)

1. **The Virtual AI Security Team (AISecOps)** — OSM is a tireless, brilliant team you "hire," composed of 5 Specialized AI Agents with the combined brainpower of ~100 top-tier analysts, working 24/7 for a fraction of the cost.
2. **The Collision Sensor for Cyber Threats** — like a car's safety system, it doesn't wait for the crash (airbag = old security); it analyzes the road, calculates collision probability, shows the crash scenario, and steers away.
