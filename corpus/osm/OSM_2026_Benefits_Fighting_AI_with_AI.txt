The new reality is defined by a simple, terrifying equation:
- 40,000+ new vulnerabilities discovered annually
- Multiplied by 4 unique layers of the attack surface (Network, Web Applications, Containers, Source Code)
- Multiplied by Hundreds, or Thousands, of connected organizational assets

The inescapable conclusion: Cybersecurity is now a Big Data problem. An organization's enemy is no longer a human hacker at a keyboard; the enemy is an AI engine processing Big Data at incomprehensible speeds. 

2. The 48-Hour Survival Window
Coupled with the Big Data problem is the hyper-accelerated speed of exploitation. As demonstrated by high-profile breaches (such as the React2Shell incident where 77,000 IPs were breached in just 2 days), the window of time to defend an organization has catastrophically shrunk.

According to Mandiant M-Trends, it now takes on average only 48 hours for threat actors to begin breaching systems after an exploit is released. 

Organizations relying on traditional, scheduled vulnerability scans (weekly, monthly, or quarterly) are effectively defenseless. If an enterprise scans on Tuesday, and an exploit drops on Wednesday, the organization will be breached by Friday—days before their next scheduled scan.
