## Industry stat library — every number to cite

### AI adoption stats
- **78%** of global companies use AI in operations.
- **90%** of organizations implementing or planning LLM use cases.
- **71%** use generative AI regularly in at least one business function.
- **2.2 billion** daily API calls across major providers.

### Security incident stats (the urgency proof points)
- **86%** of companies experienced AI security incidents in the past 12 months.
- **67%** YoY increase in AI-specific attacks (prompt injection and jailbreak volume).
- **2,500+** daily AI threats observed.
- **97%** of AI-related security breaches involved systems lacking proper access controls.
- **Prompt injection** is the **#1** OWASP AI security risk.

### Data leakage stats
- **8.5%** of prompts contain sensitive information (PII, credentials, internal data).
- **63%** of organizations report external data oversharing.
- **56%** acknowledge employees upload sensitive data to unauthorized SaaS applications.
- Average breach cost with data leakage: **$4.74M** vs. **$4.07M** without → **$670,000** difference attributed to shadow AI.
- **$4.2M** average cost per AI-related data breach (+45% annually) — datasheet hero stat.

### Shadow AI stats
- **98%** of employees use unsanctioned AI applications.
- **55%** of employees adopt SaaS applications without security involvement.
- **75%** of organizations using shadow AI lack governance policies.
- **89%** of enterprises lack purpose-built AI security.
- Average enterprise uses **67 GenAI tools, 90% unsanctioned** (cited via Prompt Security).

### Regulatory pressure stats
- **NIS2 Directive** affects **160,000+ entities** with fines up to **€10M or 2% of global turnover**.
- **EU AI Act** comprehensive requirements taking effect 2025–2026.
- **68%** of European businesses struggle with EU AI Act responsibilities.
- **40%** of IT spending in Europe goes toward compliance.

### Budget growth stats
- Cybersecurity budgets growing **9–10% annually**.
- **15%** increase in AI-specific security spending.
- **82%** plan AI-driven cybersecurity investments.

## Company size segments

### Startup / Scale-up (10–200 employees)
- Fast-moving, resource-constrained, building AI-first products
- **Needs:** Quick deployment, developer-friendly, cost-effective, pass enterprise security reviews
- **Decision:** CTO / technical founder, 1–2 week evaluation
- **Plan:** Onboard / Standard / Growth

### Mid-Market (200–2,000 employees)
- Established processes, growing AI adoption, compliance requirements emerging
- **Needs:** Policy orchestration, multi-team support, integration with existing security stack
- **Decision:** Security + Engineering + Compliance, 4–8 week evaluation
- **Plan:** Growth / Advanced

### Enterprise (2,000+ employees)
- Complex environments, strict compliance, multiple business units
- **Needs:** Enterprise governance, custom policies, dedicated support, extensive integrations
- **Decision:** Multi-stakeholder (CISO, CTO, Legal, Procurement), 3–6 month evaluation
- **Plan:** Enterprise / Enterprise+

## Geographic priorities

### Primary: European Union
- **Drivers:** NIS2, EU AI Act, GDPR, data sovereignty
- **Characteristics:** High regulatory scrutiny, risk-averse, prefer EU-based vendors
- **Approach:** Lead with compliance, emphasize data residency, reference EU customers

### Major: United States
- **Drivers:** SOC 2, HIPAA, state privacy laws, enterprise security requirements
- **Characteristics:** Fast-moving, ROI-focused, competitive market
- **Approach:** Lead with speed and developer experience, emphasize cost management

### Bridge: United Kingdom
- **Drivers:** UK GDPR, sector-specific regulations, post-Brexit compliance
- **Approach:** Balanced messaging on compliance and innovation

### Emerging: Rest of World
- APAC: data localization
- LATAM: emerging regulations
- 100+ language support enables global ambitions
