## The four voice characteristics (use all four, weighted to context)

### 1. Clear & Direct
- **No jargon unless necessary.** When jargon is required, define it briefly.
- Short sentences.
- Active voice.
- Concrete examples.
- One idea per sentence; one focus per paragraph.

### 2. Confident & Authoritative
- Backed by data and evidence (numbers, percentages, named frameworks).
- Definitive statements ("APIRE blocks 27+ threats" — not "may help block threats").
- Expert positioning.
- **No hedging or uncertainty** ("we believe", "might help", "could possibly" — avoid).

### 3. Technical but Accessible
- Explain complex concepts simply.
- Use analogies when they clarify (castle/moat, whiteboard, soap bubble — all from source material).
- Define technical terms inline.
- Respect audience intelligence. Don't oversimplify for technical readers.

### 4. Action-Oriented
- Focus on **outcomes**, not features.
- Use strong verbs (deploy, block, prevent, prove, ship).
- Clear calls-to-action.
- Emphasize speed and results.

## Messaging principles (apply to every piece)

### 1. Lead with Value
Start with the outcome, not the feature.
- ❌ "Real-time threat detection engine"
- ✅ "Block threats in real time"

### 2. Be Specific
Use numbers and metrics.
- ❌ "quickly", "many threats", "fast deployment"
- ✅ "5 minutes", "27+ threats", "100K+ attack vectors"

### 3. Avoid Hype
No "revolutionary", "game-changing", "disruptive". Let results speak. Use customer evidence and data.

### 4. Respect Technical Expertise
Don't oversimplify for technical audiences. Provide depth where appropriate. Link to detailed documentation.

### 5. Emphasize Speed & Simplicity
- "Zero code changes"
- "5-minute deployment"
- "One platform"
- "Immediate protection"

## Words and phrases to avoid

### Generic AI-marketing slop (banned)
- Synergy
- Leverage (as a verb)
- Paradigm shift
- Best-in-class, world-class, industry-leading (unless backed by evidence)
- Revolutionary
- Game-changing
- Disruptive
- Seamless (overused; almost meaningless)
- Next-generation (vague)
- Cutting-edge (without evidence)
- Robust solution (vague)
- Unlocked potential
- In today's fast-paced world

### Vague generalizations (banned)
- "Many enterprises"
- "Some companies"
- "Most CISOs agree"
- "Often"
- "Generally"

(Replace with specific numbers from `threat-coverage.md` and `market-and-industry-data.md`.)

### Soft/hedging language (avoid)
- "might"
- "could"
- "may help"
- "we believe"
- "potentially"
- "in some cases"

When uncertain about a fact, omit the claim rather than hedge.

## LinkedIn Performance Learnings
Source: Analysis of 102 published APIRE LinkedIn posts (Buffer analytics, May 2026)

Key finding: Technical-specific practitioner voice consistently outperforms broad business claims in engagement rate for APIRE's current LinkedIn audience.

WHAT WORKS (higher engagement rate):
- Specific technical actions: "swap api.openai.com to app.apire.io"
- Exact numbers: "950+ DLP rules", "27+ AI-specific threats", "sub-millisecond latency per stage"
- Practitioner-to-practitioner voice: "As practitioners, we deliver..."
- Specific regulatory framing with named deadlines: "NIS2 deadlines", "EU AI Act Article 10"

WHAT UNDERPERFORMS (lower engagement rate):
- Generic business claims: "enterprise-ready solution"
- Abstract value propositions without specific numbers
- Broad market framing without connecting to a specific action or moment

VISUAL FINDINGS:
- Images showing a person coding/working technically outperformed generic server room images in engagement rate
- Dark environment with screen-glow-as-light-source aesthetic resonates with the technical audience

## Contradictions / things to flag

- **"Process Everything. Keep Nothing." vs "Process Everything. Store Nothing.":** Both appear; prefer "Keep Nothing" in technical contexts, "Store Nothing" in marketing.
- **"4-layer" vs "5-layer" architecture in copy:** All new content should use the **5-layer** framing (Sentinel + the four detection layers). Older internal copy that says "four-layer" is not wrong about the four detection layers but is missing Sentinel/Layer 0.
- **Customer numbers / case study claims:** Source material does not include verifiable customer case studies with specific numbers (e.g., "500+ enterprises", "trusted by X bank"). Do not invent these. Reference customers (e.g., Turkish airline POC, Tier-1 European Bank) are flagged `[UNVERIFIED]` in source material — confirm with Baran before using.
